Course Description
The purpose of the American Red Cross Lifeguarding course is to provide entry-level participants with the knowledge and skills to recognize, respond
to, prevent aquatic emergencies, and provide care for breathing, cardiac emergencies, injuries and sudden illnesses until emergency medical services (EMS)
take over.



Entry-level participants must complete the following baselines:

1. Attend and actively participate in all class sessions
2. Demonstrate competency in all required skills, content, and activities
3. Pass all written exams with a minimum grade of 80%
4. Pass all in-water skill tests





Course Information
Upon completion of this course, you will be issued an American Red Cross certification for: Lifeguarding, First Aid, and CPR/AED for the
Professional Rescuer that will be valid for two years.

Course Tasks
Each week, we will complete a module (unit of study) which consists of the following structure:


The Overview - What we will learn in the module
The Content - The slideshow and webinar
The Readings - Online articles you will read based on the content
The Tasks - Assignments you will complete for the module




There are 3 types of tasks: Reading, Writing, and Participating.
Here is what the tasks will contain:



Class Stream

You will join our class stream. It is available on our class website. Your participation and writing tasks
will be submitted here. This will be our "coffee house" where we will gather to discuss what we are doing and
reading in this course, as well as help each other out with any aspects of this course. Our stream is yours
to use. This means that I expect you to be collaborating with your fellow lifeguard candidates.




Writing Activities

1. Accident-incident form
2. Textbook chapter review questions
3. First Aid/CPR/AED scenerios
4. Extension activities




Readings

In addition to the course textbook, you will read a selection of high-quality literature online on lifeguarding.
You will document what you are reading using Hypothes.is.



Video Selfies and Webinars

Video Selfies
Using an electronic device, you will be recording video selfies of yourself for different participation tasks.
Your video selfies must be listed as private and will only be viewed by me and your classmates. You may choose to use
Snapchat, FaceTime, Google Hangouts, Instagram, Skype, or any other social media tool to record yourself. Each
video selfie should not be scripted prior to your recording. You will post your video selfies and video selfie
replies to your classmates in our class stream.

Webinars
You will create slideshows using presentation tools such as Microsoft PowerPoint, Google Slides, Prezi or any
other presentation tool you would like. You will then make screencasts that teach us about the content in your slideshow
similar to mine that I do in each module. You will post your webinars and webinar responses to your classmates
in our class stream. You will also post your slideshows in our class stream.




Annotations

During this course, you will annotate the readings and reply to each others' annotations. You will use a tool called
Hypothes.is. You can make an account here. You will then join our annotation group which can be found on our class
website. You can earn extra credit by embedding your annotations in our class stream.




Exams

You will be required to take and pass two written exams and in-water skill tests which will demonstrate your
mental knowledge and physical ability that is needed to be certified as a professional lifeguard. Per the American Red
Cross, failure to show up for exams or failing exams after the allowed number of retests and retries will result
in retaking the entire course. You are allowed 1 re-test for each written exam and 3 retries for each
in-water skill tests.
